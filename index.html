  • Ask ChatGPT: What is New York Pizza?

    New York-style pizza is a style of pizza characterized by large, hand-tossed thin crusts and a liberal use of tomato sauce and mozzarella cheese. It's a derivative of the original Neapolitan pizza from Italy but has been adapted to meet the tastes and preferences of New Yorkers. This style of pizza is typically cooked in a gas oven, which contributes to its distinct crisp edge and slightly chewy center.

    A hallmark of New York-style pizza is its flexibility; the slices are large and wide with a thin crust that is foldable yet crispy on the edges. This makes it easy to eat on the go, which suits the fast-paced lifestyle of New York City. Toppings can vary widely, but the classic New York slice features a simple layer of tomato sauce and shredded mozzarella cheese. Additional toppings are often layered on this base, including pepperoni, sausage, mushrooms, onions, and bell peppers.

    New York-style pizza is served in both pizzerias and by the slice, a nod to the city's bustling street food scene. The ability to buy a single slice of pizza and eat it as street food is a defining characteristic of New York's pizza culture. This style of pizza has become immensely popular across the United States and has influenced the development of other regional pizza styles.
